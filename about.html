ABOUT THE NETWORK

Building Africa's Algae Innovation Ecosystem

The Algae Africa Network (AAN) is Africa's continental platform dedicated to connecting researchers, educators, entrepreneurs, policymakers, industry leaders and development partners to unlock the enormous potential of algae for sustainable development.

Who We Are

The Algae Africa Network (AAN) is a professional network established to promote collaboration, knowledge sharing, innovation and commercialization within Africa's algae sector.

AAN brings together scientists, students, universities, government agencies, private companies, entrepreneurs, investors and development organizations working across microalgae and seaweed sector.

Our goal is to position Africa as a global leader in algae research, biotechnology and sustainable innovation.

Our Vision

To build a thriving African algae ecosystem that drives scientific excellence, environmental sustainability, food security, climate resilience, innovation and inclusive economic growth.

Our Mission

Connecting Africa's algae community through research, education, innovation and partnerships.

Research

Promote collaborative scientific research, knowledge generation and innovation across Africa.

Education

Provide learning opportunities, capacity building, training, webinars and educational resources.

Partnerships

Strengthen collaboration between academia, industry, government, investors and development partners.

Our Strategic Objectives

Advance Research

Strengthen algae science, innovation and technology development.

Food Security

Promote algae applications in food, feed, nutrition and agriculture.

Climate Action

Support climate resilience, carbon capture, ecosystem restoration and wastewater treatment.

Blue Economy

Encourage entrepreneurship, commercialization, investment and sustainable industries.

Why Africa?

Africa's algae future is not limited by biology. It is shaped by how well we connect science, entrepreneurship, industry and communities. With thousands of kilometres of coastline, vast freshwater resources, diverse climates and exceptional biodiversity, Africa possesses the natural foundations for a thriving algae economy. The next opportunity is to transform these resources into research, businesses, manufacturing, sustainable livelihoods and climate solutions built in Africa. That is the future AAN exists to help build.

Through regional collaboration, Africa can become a global leader in algae biotechnology, renewable bio-products, aquaculture, food production, environmental restoration, renewable energy and the blue bioeconomy.
